# brief insights

## dataset overview
- imported the sample superstore dataset into mysql successfully.
- each record represents a product purchased in an order.
- dataset contains information about orders, customers, products, sales and profit.

## filtering insights
- technology products showed higher sales values compared to many other products.
- sales and orders are distributed across different regions and states.
- only a limited number of orders have very high sales values.

## aggregation insights
- technology category generated the highest revenue.
- office supplies had a high number of orders because of lower-priced products.
- average sales varied significantly among different categories.

## top products and customers
- a few products contributed a major share of total sales.
- some customers placed significantly larger orders than others.
- top customers generated a substantial portion of overall revenue.

## monthly trends
- sales changed from month to month and showed seasonal variations.
- certain periods experienced higher sales, indicating increased customer demand.

## data quality checks
- total row count was verified after importing the dataset.
- missing and null values were checked to ensure data consistency.
- duplicate order ids were expected because one order can contain multiple products.

## conclusion
the sql analysis helped in understanding sales performance, customer behaviour and product trends. these insights can support better business decisions related to inventory, marketing and customer management.
